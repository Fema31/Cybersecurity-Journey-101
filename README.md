## 🌟 Inspiration: Giants Upon Whose Shoulders I Stand

My journey is fueled by the legacy of those who paved the way. I am particularly inspired by the brilliance and resilience of pioneering women in science.

---

### 🏛️ Fatima al-Fihri
> **"Knowledge is a trust. What we learn, we owe to the world.”**

**Fatima al-Fihri**, a 9th-century Muslim woman, is a foundational figure in the history of education. After inheriting her father's wealth, she channeled it into building a center of learning for her community. She founded the **University of al-Qarawiyyin** in Fez, Morocco, in **859 AD**—recognized by UNESCO and the Guinness World Records as the **oldest existing and continually operating educational institution in the world**. This university became a legendary beacon, attracting scholars from across the globe to study sciences, astronomy, languages, and law. It was pivotal in spreading knowledge, such as introducing Arabic numerals to the Western world. Her story is a powerful reminder that the core of science and cybersecurity is **sharing knowledge to uplift everyone**.

### ⚗️ Marie Curie
> **"I am among those who think that science has great beauty."**

**Marie Curie**, a physicist and chemist, was a force of nature whose work fundamentally changed the world. Despite facing immense challenges and working in a rudimentary lab, her relentless curiosity led to the discovery of **polonium and radium** and pioneered the study of **radioactivity**—a term she herself coined. She is the first person and only woman to win Nobel Prizes in **two different scientific fields** (Physics and Chemistry). Her work, which ultimately contributed to her death from radiation exposure, was driven by a profound love for discovery. She is rightly called the **'mother of modern physics'**. Her life teaches me that profound beauty and groundbreaking discovery lie in dedication and asking the right questions, no matter the obstacles.

### 🧠 Mentor's Advice: The SOC Starter Pack

> **"In Cybersecurity, you will most likely start in a SOC (Security Operations Center). Your goal is to become the analyst that every team wants to hire."**

Here is the essential knowledge and toolset to focus on to achieve that goal:

#### 🌐 Foundational Knowledge (Non-Negotiable)
*   **NETWORKING:** Understand how data moves. Learn **TCP/IP, DNS, HTTP/S, ports, and protocols**. This is the **#1 most important skill** for any security role.
*   **CYBERSECURITY BASICS:** Master the core principles: the **CIA Triad**, threat actors, and common attacks (phishing, malware, DDoS).
*   **TROUBLESHOOTING:** Cultivate a methodical approach to diagnosing and solving problems. This is a core analyst mindset.

#### 🔧 Essential Tools & Technologies
*   **Operating Systems:** Become fluent in both **Windows and Linux** command lines.
*   **Security Suites:** Learn how modern defenses work:
    *   **NDR (Network Detection & Response):** Monitors network traffic for threats.
    *   **EDR (Endpoint Detection & Response):** Monitors endpoints (laptops, servers) for malicious activity.
    *   **SIEM (Security Information & Event Management):** The SOC's central brain; aggregates and analyzes logs from all systems.
*   **Kali Linux Suite:** The go-to toolkit for security professionals.
    *   `Nmap` - The network mapper
    *   `Wireshark` - Network protocol analyzer
    *   `Burp Suite` - Web application security testing
    *   `Metasploit` - Penetration testing framework
    *   `John the Ripper` - Password cracking
    *   `Hydra` - Network logon cracker
*   **Open-Source Intelligence (OSINT) Tools:**
    *   **VirusTotal** - Analyzes suspicious files and URLs.
    *   **Shodan** - Search engine for Internet-connected devices.
    *   **CISCO Talos** - Threat intelligence resource.
    *   **Exploit Database** - Archive of exploits and vulnerabilities.

#### 📜 Governance, Risk & Compliance (GRC)
*   **Frameworks:** Learn the main frameworks used in your region, such as:
    *   **GDPR** (General Data Protection Regulation)
    *   **ISO 27001** (International standard for information security management)

#### 💼 Underrated Professional Skills
*   **Productivity Suites:** Develop advanced skills in **Microsoft 365** or **Google Workspace**. Understanding data flows and security settings in these platforms is crucial.
*   **Reporting:** Learn how to write clear, concise, and high-quality reports. Use tools like **Canva** for visual presentations and explore **specialized AI assistants** to help draft and refine content.

## ✅ Must-Haves for Entry-Level (First 1–2 Years)

These are the **core skills employers expect** for SOC Analyst, Junior Cybersecurity Analyst, or IT Security Support roles.

### 🔧 Core Tech
- `Linux` – Command line comfort
- `Bash` – Basic scripting & automation
- `Networking` – OSI model, TCP/IP, ports, protocols
- `Python` – Simple scripts, automation, log parsing
- `Windows Basics` – Active Directory, users/permissions
- `Virtualization` – Set up labs (VirtualBox, VMware, Hyper-V)
- `OWASP Top 10` – Web security fundamentals

### 🛡️ Defense (SOC Foundations)
- `SIEM` (Splunk, Wazuh, Elastic)
- `SOC Basics` (alert triage, log analysis, ticketing)
- `Incident Response` (playbooks for phishing, malware, brute force)

### 📊 Visualization
- `Wireshark` – Packet analysis & traffic investigation
